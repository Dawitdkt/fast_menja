## Motorway Rules and Procedures

Motorways are high-speed dual carriageways designed for fast, long-distance travel. They have specific rules and procedures that you **must** follow.

### Speed limits on motorways:

- **Cars**: 70 mph (112 km/h)
- **Vans/small vehicles**: 70 mph
- **HGVs**: 60 mph (96 km/h)
- **These are maximum speeds** - drive slower in bad weather, traffic, or if you feel unsafe

### Lane discipline:

1. **Nearside (left) lane** - Main lane for driving
2. **Middle lane(s)** - For overtaking and faster traffic
3. **Offside (right) lane** - For overtaking only

### Keep to the left rule:

- Keep in the left lane unless overtaking
- Return to the left lane once you've finished overtaking
- Don't sit in the middle or right lanes

### Overtaking procedure:

1. Check mirrors and blind spots
2. Signal your intention
3. Move to the next lane
4. Overtake safely
5. Check mirrors
6. Signal left and move back to the left lane

### Joining a motorway:

- Use the acceleration lane to build up speed
- Time your merge to fit into traffic gaps
- Don't force your way into traffic

### Leaving a motorway:

- Follow signs for at least 1 mile beforehand
- Use the deceleration lane (right lane)
- Don't reverse if you miss your exit

### Motorway hazards:

- High speed reduces your reaction time
- Weather conditions affect grip
- Vehicle breakdowns are dangerous
- Fatigue is a major risk factor

### Essential rules:

- **No learner drivers** - Must hold full license
- **Hard shoulder** - Emergency use only
- **Contraflow** - Never drive against traffic direction
- **Stopping** - Not allowed except in emergencies
- **Pedestrians** - Not allowed on motorways

### Breakdowns and emergencies:

- Try to reach a service station
- Use the hard shoulder only as a last resort
- Turn off at the next exit
- Call recovery or emergency services
- Stay in your vehicle away from traffic

Remember: Motorways are for experienced drivers only. Never exceed speed limits and always prioritize safety over speed.
